# ProofSignals Strategy

## Core Recommendation

Launch **ProofSignals** as a **member-first founder feedback network** with **optional public profile pages** introduced later.

This gives the product the best balance of:
- honest human-to-human feedback
- privacy during early interactions
- safer trust and moderation controls
- future SEO and shareable reputation pages
- stronger brand coherence than splitting the product across unrelated domains

## Positioning

**ProofSignals** should be positioned as:

> A trusted founder-to-founder feedback and reputation network for marketers, builders, consultants, creators, and B2B operators.

The product should emphasize:
- meaningful human feedback
- intelligent matching
- credibility and trust signals
- private collaboration first
- optional public proof later

The product should not lead with:
- review swapping
- engagement exchanges
- “five-star” energy
- public rating farming

## Why Not Fully Public at Launch

A fully public model creates avoidable early-stage problems:
- low-quality early profiles look weak in search
- moderation burden rises significantly
- users become less candid if everything may become public
- many users will not want unfinished assets indexed by search engines
- the app may start to resemble a public review platform too early

## Why Not Stay Fully Private Forever

A totally private product loses several strong benefits:
- no searchable proof of credibility
- no public trust pages users can share
- weaker SEO and organic acquisition
- less visible reputation value for power users

## Best Direction

Use a **hybrid model**:
- private/member-only core product
- optional public profiles
- optional public asset pages
- strong publishing controls
- public visibility turned off by default

## Brand Recommendation

Use **ProofSignals** as the single master brand.

Recommended brand structure:
- ProofSignals — main brand
- ProofSignals Network — member platform
- ProofSignals Profiles — public optional profiles
- ProofSignals Market — founder marketplace
- ProofSignals Feedback — internal review system

## Domain Recommendation

Use one coherent brand and domain structure.

Recommended pattern:
- `app.[domain]` for the private member application
- `[domain]/[username]` for public profiles
- `[domain]/market` for marketplace pages later

Avoid using a disconnected public brand such as `fivestarz.net` for the front-facing side of the product.

### Why avoid split brands
- trust gets diluted
- users may be confused
- SEO authority gets split
- the public brand may feel gimmicky or manipulative
- “five star” framing conflicts with the product’s ethical, thoughtful feedback positioning

## Product Philosophy

The strongest version of ProofSignals is:

> AI helps users present assets better. Humans provide the feedback. Public proof is optional and earned.

That means:
- AI helps generate asset listings
- AI helps classify feedback requests
- AI helps summarize patterns across human feedback
- AI does not write feedback or testimonials for users

## Monetization Direction

Free users can receive most of the value of the network while paid users unlock deeper control and visibility.

Suggested paid features:
- marketplace listing privileges
- more matches
- advanced matching filters
- hidden identity / blind-first mode
- public profile publishing tools
- public video showcases
- advanced feedback analytics
- degrees-of-separation trust controls

## Long-Term Moat

ProofSignals becomes defensible if it owns:
- reviewer reputation
- trust-quality scoring
- category expertise mapping
- feedback pattern intelligence
- ethical founder reputation pages
- a curated founder-to-founder marketplace

## Bottom Line

Launch private-first.
Add public reputation gradually.
Keep the brand unified.
Use **ProofSignals** as the umbrella, not a split “serious backend / gimmicky public frontend” setup.
